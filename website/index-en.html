FAQ

You might be wondering

Can GUARDIAN diagnose illness?

No — and it shouldn't. GUARDIAN provides objective data records and statistical-deviation nudges; these are health prompts, not a medical diagnosis. All diagnosis and treatment should come from a licensed vet. Our value is making sure you don't miss the moment worth asking, and that the vet sees continuous data instead of a single snapshot.

Is the collar useful without a subscription?

Absolutely. Live location, geofencing and route playback are free for life — as a good-looking anti-loss collar it already pays for itself. Membership unlocks the vital-sign trends and guardianship services.

Will the nudges be annoying?

That's exactly what we care about most. The system only nudges when a trend persistently crosses a statistical threshold; single blips are logged silently, not pushed. We'd rather say less than cry wolf.

Is my pet's data safe?

Data is tiered: basic activity, private location, and sensitive physiological data are encrypted and access-controlled separately. You fully own the data and can export or revoke authorization anytime; any third party (vet/insurer) can only access the relevant time window when you explicitly authorize a service.

How long does the battery last?

An edge-computing power strategy: it sleeps when still and wakes for high-rate sampling only on activity or anomalies, so a single charge lasts multiple days (final figures at launch).

Can cats wear it?

GUARDIAN ONE launches for dogs first, in collar and harness forms. A lightweight cat version is on the roadmap.
